Balance Sheet and Capital Allocation

Net debt at 30 September 2025 stood at €2.14 billion, representing a net leverage ratio of 1.77× EBITDA (LTM). This is up from 1.23× at the start of the year, reflecting the €1.1 billion cash consideration for DeepStor closed in July. The company expects leverage to return below 1.5× by Q2 2026 through free cash flow generation without further material M&A.

The Board has approved an additional €500 million share repurchase programme, effective from 1 January 2026, supplementing the €300 million programme announced in April 2025 (€247 million of which has been executed). The quarterly dividend of €0.41 per share is maintained, implying a full-year dividend of €1.64, up from €1.40 in fiscal 2024.

DeepStor Integration Update

The acquisition of DeepStor, a specialist in cold-storage data archival infrastructure, closed on 15 July 2025 for a total enterprise value of €1.34 billion (10.8× LTM EBITDA). Integration is proceeding ahead of schedule: cross-selling into the Nordveld enterprise customer base has generated €31 million of incremental pipeline in the first 3.5 months. Cost synergies of €45 million (annualised) are now expected to be realised by Q2 2026, six months ahead of the original timeline.

DeepStor's proprietary cold-tier storage technology is being integrated into the Nordveld Cloud Infrastructure platform as the "Archival Tier," targeted at financial services, healthcare, and media customers with regulatory data retention requirements. The company expects this product to contribute meaningfully to cloud revenue from Q1 2026.

Risks and Outlook

Management notes three principal risk factors for Q4: (1) Macroeconomic uncertainty in the German and French enterprise markets, where procurement cycles have lengthened; (2) Currency headwinds if the USD weakens below 1.15 against the EUR; (3) Competitive pricing pressure in the managed services segment from hyperscalers with aggressive European expansion strategies. Against these, the company notes strong visibility from its SaaS recurring revenue base (62% of total revenue) and the backlog of signed but not-yet-commenced cloud infrastructure contracts (€1.8 billion at end of Q3).
